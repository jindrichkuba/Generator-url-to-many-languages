  • Bạn có phải là người dễ gần không?

Các Cuộc Trò chuyện Quan trọng





Cuộc trò chuyện Quan trọng là gì?
Cuộc trò chuyện quan trọng gây tác động đến một trong hai bên hoặc hầu như tác động đến cả hai bên và doanh nghiệp. Thường xuyên:


  • Có rủi ro cao

  • Có thể có quan điểm trái chiều

  • Mang đến cảm xúc mãnh liệt

  • Gây ra cảm giác không thoải mái

Tại sao Cần có Cuộc trò chuyện Quan trọng?

Hoạt động Giám sát TUYỆT VỜI yêu cầu chúng ta quản lý thành công Nhiệm vụ, Đội ngũ và Cá nhân. Để thực hiện việc này, đôi khi cần phải có cuộc trò chuyện quan trọng.

Bài tập thực hành sẽ giúp bạn xác định những lý do khiến chúng ta phải có cuộc trò chuyện và lý do khiến đôi khi chúng ta không cần cuộc trò chuyện.

Để bắt đầu, hãy nghĩ đến hoạt động Chuẩn bị của bạn và những mối quan hệ khó khăn hơn đó hay khi bạn phải nói Không hoặc đưa ra một số phản hồi khó khăn. Cơ hội chính là các cuộc trò chuyện quan trọng.

Getting Real with Relationships

This activity will give you an opportunity – in a safe environment – to practice how you would handle a challenging situation or crucial conversation in order to build a better relationship with the other person.


In your Pre-work and throughout this workshop, you have identified and examined relationships that would benefit from that opportunity. Now is your chance to try out your new skills and strategies and gain some feedback.


Working in groups of three or four, you will each play the role of Supervisor (A), Colleague (B) and Observers (C).
Here, ‘Colleague’ means any person you may need to speak to e.g. colleague, team member, Manager, Customer etc.


When you are in the role of the Supervisor (A), you will have 15 minutes to practice conducting a crucial conversation with the Colleague (B), followed by 5 minutes feedback from your Observer (C). Rotate roles.

Activity Instructions

Complete a ‘Real Play’ brief for the person playing your Colleague (B) so they can make the practice as realistic as possible.

When you are the Observer (C), you will need to complete an Observer Feedback form to assist with the feedback.

Consider how you want to start and close the conversation; remember Words, Music and Dance.

Appoint a timekeeper to keep your group on track. You can record your practice to review later.
Capture any hints, tips and feedback from this practice, as there will be an opportunity to share them later.
Good luck!

Human Sigma

INTRODUCTION

To some degree or another, most businesses seeking a viable long-term future want to understand the quality or value they are offering their customers. For some businesses, this is easier to achieve than others either by the nature of their activity or scale of operation. For example, businesses have a pretty good idea nowadays how to judge the effectiveness and quality of manufacturing processes, say to produce an exciting, innovative, new hand-held device, but less so the effectiveness and quality of a retail employee’s attempts to sell it or the call centre employee’s efforts to help a customer navigate its function buttons. Since the 1980s, Six Sigma and other TQM (Total Quality Management) approaches have delivered huge benefits to manufacturing and other businesses with predictable systems, processes and products. However, for the growing sales and service sectors (where there is a higher human element) measuring quality/value has proven much harder until now.

GALLUP RESEARCH

Gallup, the leading, global business research company, has created a way of measuring this all-important aspect of performance they call ‘Human Sigma’. Their research grew out of a multi-year, research-based initiative designed to understand all aspects of the employeecustomer encounter. They identified ways to measure the effectiveness of the encounter, explored how those metrics could best be used, and assessed the benefits that could result from their application. Their work was based on direct experience with hundreds of companies and millions of customers and employees. They then tested and cross-validated their findings in 1,979 business units – involved in financial services, professional services, retail and sales – within 10 companies. The results to date have been extraordinary. The 10 companies, all of which have applied the best practice principles for managing the employee-customer encounter, together outperformed their five largest competitors during 2003 by 26% in gross margins and by 85% in sales growth.

1/6

HUMAN SIGMA RESEARCH FINDINGS

EMPLOYEE ENGAGEMENT IS ESSENTIAL FOR CUSTOMER ENGAGEMENT

Engaged employees (i.e. those who have an emotional connection to and feel involved with the business they work for) have higher levels of productivity and profitability, better safety and attendance records, and higher levels of retention. Not surprisingly, they’re also more effective at engaging the customers they serve. Disengaged employees have a profound impact too. Gallup estimate that they cost companies $300 billion per year in lost productivity in the United States alone. They also destroy customer relationships with remarkable ease, day in and day out.

CUSTOMER ENGAGEMENT DELIVERS IMPROVED BOTTOM LINE RESULTS

“Extremely satisfied” customers (people who provide the highest rating of overall satisfaction with a company’s products and services) fall into two distinct groups: those who have a strong emotional connection to the company and those who do not. When Gallup examined indicators of customer behavior (such as attrition, frequency of use, total revenue, and total spending), a clear and striking pattern emerged. Emotionally satisfied customers contribute far more to the bottom line than rationally satisfied customers do, even though they both rate the company equally. In fact, the behavior of rationally satisfied customers looks no different from that of dissatisfied customers. Research suggests that for all kinds of companies, emotionally engaged customers deliver a 23% premium over the average customer in terms of share of wallet, profitability, revenue, and relationship growth. Actively disengaged customers represent a 13% discount on the same measures. Within a given company, business units whose levels of customer engagement are in the top 25% tend to outperform all other units on measures of profit contribution, sales and growth by a factor of 2:1.

2/6

HUMAN SIGMA RESEARCH FINDINGS

ANY PERFORMANCE MEASURE NEEDS TO TAKE CUSTOMERS’ EMOTIONS INTO ACCOUNT

Given that people’s decisions have been proven to be a mix of emotion and reason, Gallup developed a measure of customer engagement to reflect this. It combines traditional metrics of customer loyalty (overall satisfaction, likelihood to repurchase and likelihood to recommend) with a short set of items that assesses the emotional nature of customers’ commitment.

  • 1ST DIMENSION CONFIDENCE
    Does this company always deliver on its promises? Are its people competent?
  • 2ND DIMENSION INTEGRITY
    Does this company treat me the way I deserve to be treated? If something goes awry, can I count on the company to fix it fast?
  • 3RD DIMENSION PRIDE
    Do I feel a sense of positive identification with the company?
  • 4TH DIMENSION PASSION
    Is the company irreplaceable in my life and a perfect fit for me?

In summary, Gallup found there is a correlation between a business’s ability to create emotional engagement and its financial performance - the stronger the levels of emotional engagement, the better the financial results are likely to be.

3/6

HUMAN SIGMA RESEARCH RECOMMENDATIONS

MANAGERS NEED TO CREATE EMPLOYEE ENGAGEMENT

Employee engagement is not a company’s or manager’s automatic right, so managers need to work hard to create the right conditions for employee engagement to happen. Extensive research has proven employee engagement levels increase when managers behave consistently and invest their time in at least 12 employee-related activities. Called the Gallup 12, managers need to:

  1. Ensure their team knows what is expected of them at work
  2. Provide the materials and equipment their team needs to do their job right
  3. Create opportunities for their team to do what they do best every day
  4. Have given recognition and praise to their team for doing good work in the last seven days
  5. Show that they care for their team as individual people
  6. Actively encourage their team’s development
  7. Show their team their opinions count
  8. Take time to explain how individual/team activity fits with the Company mission/purpose
  9. Ensure everyone in their team is committed to doing quality work
  10. Create the kind of work environment where friendships develop amongst the team
  11. Have talked to individuals in their team about their progress at work in the last six months
  12. Have created opportunities at work for their team to learn and grow in the last year

HUMAN SIGMA RESEARCH RECOMMENDATIONS

THE ENCOUNTER MUST BE MEASURED LOCALLY

This is because averages or summary headline results cloud what is happening locally. This would be like your doctor ignoring your own heart rate and giving you a diagnosis based on the average heart rate of your entire town! Averages obscure the considerable variation from location to location within a company. Therefore, they don’t give managers and executives the information they need to improve performance.

RESPONSIBILITY FOR HUMAN SIGMA MUST BE CENTRALIZED

Since employee and customer engagement are intimately connected and since, taken together, they have a significant effect on financial performance, they need to be managed holistically (at an organizational and local level). Unfortunately, that is easier said than done as in many businesses customer data is in Marketing, employee data is with HR and financial data lives in Finance. However, only when these are brought together on a single platform can a true picture of the health of the employee-customer encounter be drawn.

APPOINT AN EXECUTIVE SPONSOR

To ensure Human Sigma has meaning, it is important that the responsibility for measuring and monitoring the health of the employee-customer relationship resides with a single organizational structure, with an Executive champion who can initiate and drive change. Providing managers with a ‘dashboard’ of seemingly unrelated gages and dials drawn from various and dispersed quarters of the organization is not enough to drive change.

5/6

HUMAN SIGMA RESEARCH RECOMMENDATIONS

THE LOCAL MANAGER IS THE SINGLE MOST IMPORTANT FACTOR IN LOCAL GROUP PERFORMANCE

Local-level managers have a huge role to play, for better or worse, in local performance. Earlier Gallup research suggested that employees join great companies, but leave poor managers. That is, employees join a company for a variety of both high-minded and practical reasons. However, their working lives revolve around local environments that can either nourish them and foster their learning OR starve them, causing them ultimately to leave the company – or to hang around, unproductively waiting for retirement. Local managers whose work groups show lower levels of performance should be encouraged to use the familiar tool kit of interventions: targeted training, performance reviews, action learning, and individual coaching. These managers themselves should be supported in similar ways. If none of these interventions lead to better performance, the local manager should be replaced.

SOME COMPANIES WILL NEED TO OVERHAUL THEIR HR PRACTICES

If Human Sigma results are lower than expected, businesses may need to look at short-term and longer- term, transformational interventions. Short-term e.g. how it selects employees, promotes people into management, does performance appraisals, approaches succession planning, and recognises performance etc. Longer-term e.g. re-examining how local leaders do their jobs, how these managers are being developed, and decisions are made and executed at the local level.
